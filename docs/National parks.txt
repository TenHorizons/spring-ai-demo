Introduction to National Parks
* National Parks are protected areas of land designated by a national government for conservation, education, and recreation.
* There are currently 500 National Parks in the United States (as of 2011).
* The first National Park, Yellowstone, was established in 1872.
* National Parks encompass a variety of ecosystems, including mountains, forests, deserts, glaciers, and coastlines.
* They are home to a diverse range of plants and animals, many of which are endangered or threatened.
Key Features of National Parks (Bullet Points)
* Scenic beauty and natural wonders
* Diverse plant and animal life
* Opportunities for outdoor recreation (hiking, camping, fishing, etc.)
* Historical and cultural significance
* Educational resources and visitor centers
* Importance for conservation and environmental protection
Page 2: Exploring Yosemite National Park
* Yosemite National Park is located in the Sierra Nevada mountains of California.
* It is famous for its granite cliffs, waterfalls, giant sequoia trees, and clear lakes.
* Popular activities in Yosemite include hiking, rock climbing, camping, and photography.
* Some notable landmarks in Yosemite include:
   * Half Dome
   * El Capitan
   * Yosemite Falls (one of the tallest waterfalls in the world)
   * Giant Sequoia Grove (home to some of the largest trees on Earth)
   * Glacier Point (offering panoramic views of the park)
Threats to National Parks (Bullet Points)
* Climate change
* Habitat loss
* Invasive species
* Air and water pollution
* Overcrowding and overuse
* Importance of sustainable practices for park conservation
